Everything else has gone dim or gone home.

The fog has moved in like a tenant

who knows he was not invited

but plans to stay for as long as the night agrees.

But here —

between the shut shop and the empty road —

one lamp, flickering.

Not confident. Not steady.

Flickering the way a person flickers

at two in the morning

when they are not sure they can keep going —

but they do.

They do anyway.

It is not a strong light.

You could not read by it.

You could not navigate a ship.

But if you were walking alone

through the thick of a night

that had decided to become opacity,

and you saw it —

amber through the grey,

beating its uncertain beat —

you would walk toward it.

You would not even decide to.

You would simply find yourself

moving toward the one thing

that was still trying.

That is all it takes, sometimes.

Not brightness.

Not certainty.

Just: still there.

Still on.

Still amber in the grey.

Still, for reasons you may never know,

staying.
