There was a time I filled the house with sound —

the radio, the TV, anything

to cover up the silence and its sting,

that echo where the voices had been found.

I thought the quiet meant that I was wrong,

that empty rooms were evidence of lack,

that every person's absence was an attack

on proof that I belonged, that I was strong.

But silence teaches those who sit inside it.

It is not empty — it is full of me:

the breath, the hum, the life I've tried to hide it

from myself. I am my own company.

The house is still. The house is mine tonight.

I pour one glass. I turn down all the light.
