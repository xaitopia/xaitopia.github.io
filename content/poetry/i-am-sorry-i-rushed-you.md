You were sitting at the bottom of the stairs.

The ones in the house we used to live in.

You were wearing the yellow dress.

I recognised the dress before I recognised you —

and then I recognised everything.

You looked at me the way only you can look at me:

without accusation, without performance, just — waiting.

The way children wait when they have learned

that certain adults will eventually arrive.

I sat down beside you on the step.

I could not look at you directly at first.

I said: I am sorry.

You did not say: for what?

You already knew for what.

You had been waiting for this for a very long time.

I am sorry I told you to be quieter.

I am sorry I made you small enough to fit.

I am sorry I was so afraid of what you needed

that I called your needs inconvenient.

You deserved someone who sat with you then.

You are sitting with me now.

I will not rush you.

We have as long as this dream allows.
