It arrived on a Tuesday containing a refrigerator.

By Wednesday it contained the universe.

We cut a door with blunt scissors and a window with a butter knife

and called it engineering.

We drew the control panel in crayon —

red for thrust, blue for oxygen,

a dial that said SPEED with an arrow pointing to a number

we had invented ourselves

because the real ones were not fast enough.

We climbed in.

And here is the thing about a child inside a box:

they are not pretending.

They have not learned yet that pretending and believing

are different things.

They go where they go.

They see what they see.

We launched at 14:00 hours on a Saturday

in the rainy season.

We were gone for three hours.

When we came back, our mother was calling us for dinner.

We climbed out of the box.

The box was still a box.

But we had been to places that do not appear on maps

because maps are made by people

who forgot that a cardboard door

opens onto anything

if you believe it does.
