You sit across from me as if no time has passed.

The tea is hot. The room is nearly still.

I thought you were a child's dream, unsurpassed —

but here you are, and here I am, and will.

The tea is hot. The room is nearly still.

I made two cups without meaning to tonight.

But here you are, and here I am, and will —

some parts of us refuse to leave the light.

I made two cups without meaning to tonight.

The hand remembered what the mind forgot.

Some parts of us refuse to leave the light;

you were the self who kept me when I could not.

The hand remembered what the mind forgot —

you held the door when I was too afraid.

You were the self who kept me when I could not.

I thought you were a child's dream — you stayed.

You held the door when I was too afraid.

I thought you were a child's dream, unsurpassed.

I thought you were a child's dream — you stayed.

You sit across from me as if no time has passed.
