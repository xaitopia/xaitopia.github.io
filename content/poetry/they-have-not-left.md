At the junction where two roads meet in the dark, they are with me.

Before I know the danger, something shifts — they are with me.

My grandmother's hands are not hands anymore, but they are with me.

In the ache of a familiar smell on a strange street, they are with me.

The elders poured libation so the line would not be broken —

across every border I have ever crossed, they are with me.

I felt it once in a room where everything went wrong and then didn't.

Something turned the outcome. In that turning, they were with me.

The Yoruba say: àwọn àgbà ò ti lọ — the ancestors have not gone.

This is what they mean: they are with me.

I do not always feel them. But I felt them then.

And when I could not find myself, they were with me.
