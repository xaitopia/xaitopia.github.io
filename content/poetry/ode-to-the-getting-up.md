Nobody will give you a medal for this.

There will be no ceremony.

The world will not pause to acknowledge

that this morning, against the full weight of yourself,

you sat up.

But I am here.

And I am telling you: I saw it.

I saw the three times you almost didn't.

I saw the negotiation with the ceiling,

the way you lay there taking stock of what you had

and whether it was enough to justify the vertical.

And then you got up.

Not for any grand reason.

Maybe just because the bladder is not sympathetic to depression.

Maybe just because some quiet instinct,

older than your sadness, older than your reasons,

said: not today.

Not today the giving in.

That is not nothing.

That is not a small thing dressed up as a large one.

That is the whole of it, some days —

the getting up,

the putting on,

the walking out into the world

as if you had intended to all along.

Nobody will give you a medal.

But I see you.

I know what it cost.

And I am telling you: it counted.

It always counted.
