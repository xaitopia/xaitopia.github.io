The boxes lean like weary soldiers now,

The ghosts of lost deposits laid to rest.

I wipe the grit from off a tired brow,

And end the long and hollow, rental quest.

No more the scuffs I wasn't told to make,

Or painting dreams in shades I didn't choose;

The "temporary" feel is mine to break,

I'm finished with a life of borrowed views.

 

The key is cold, a heavy, silver weight,

It bites the palm with such a holy sting.

The tumblers turn — a small, metallic gate —

And oh, the music that the hinges sing!

A sanctuary carved from wood and stone;

I turn the light on in a world of own.
