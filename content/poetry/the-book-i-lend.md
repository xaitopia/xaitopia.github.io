I lend you this the way I'd lend a key

to the one room where all my best things stay —

the margins hold the person I used to be,

each dog-eared page a record of a day.

 

The underlines are questions I once asked,

the pencilled notes a younger, louder voice.

The spine is soft from years of being clasped.

I lend it knowing it was not a choice

 

but trust — the rarest currency I know —

that says: here is the proof of what I've loved,

the evidence of where my mind would go.

Return it when you're ready. I have shoved

 

aside the fear that giving this might mean

you'll know me by the spaces in between.
