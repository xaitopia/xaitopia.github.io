It was not a dramatic moment.

No sunrise. No song that swelled.

It was a Tuesday.

It was a cup of tea gone cold on the kitchen counter

while I stood at the window watching a small bird

argue with a larger bird over something in the yard.

And I laughed.

Just a small one.

The kind that arrives before you've cleared it.

The kind the body produces without asking permission.

And in the space after the laugh I felt it:

still there.

Still going.

That old stubborn muscle in its old stubborn cage,

still doing the one thing it was built to do —

not for anyone,

not because the hurt was over,

not because the missing had stopped —

just because beating is what it does.

I stood there a little longer.

The birds resolved their dispute.

The tea was cold.

And I was still here.

And that, I thought,

is something.

That, it turns out,

is quite a lot.
