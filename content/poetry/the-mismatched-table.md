We set the table, and we lit the light.

The chairs don't match; the tablecloth is old.

We are each other's family tonight.

 

No bloodline said we'd share this winter night,

no ancestry, no story we were told.

We set the table, and we lit the light.

 

The candles drip through hours burning bright,

the gravy bowl passed warm against the cold.

We are each other's family tonight.

 

Someone is weeping, and it is alright —

a hand extends before the hurt grows bold.

We set the table, and we lit the light.

 

No document gave us each other's sight,

yet here we are, the ones who chose this fold.

We are each other's family tonight.

 

So fill your plate and hold your candle tight,

there's room for all the lost, the young, the old.

We set the table, and we lit the light.

We are each other's family tonight.
