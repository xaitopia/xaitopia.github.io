O cloth of many stories,

O stitched and gathered testament —

you are not a blanket.

You are a record.

This square, blue and gold:

from the aso-ebi of an aunt's wedding

in a year before I was born.

She was twenty-two. She was beautiful.

The photograph confirms it,

but this cloth confirms it more,

because a photograph only captures what was there.

The cloth was worn against the skin.

It knows something the camera doesn't.

This square, faded green:

I recognise it.

It is from a dress I remember.

The dress she wore on Sundays

when she was dressed for God and would not sit

until after service,

would not let you touch her,

would not let you climb into her lap

until the church clothes were folded away.

Then she would let you.

Then she was all softness and warmth

and the smell of Pears soap and something older,

something that had no name,

something that simply meant: home.

O wrapper of many pieces —

you are the archive they did not think to keep.

You are the proof that they were here,

that they dressed for occasions,

that they were young once,

that they loved in the way of cloth and stitch

and Sunday afternoons.

I will keep you.

I will not wash out what remains of them.
