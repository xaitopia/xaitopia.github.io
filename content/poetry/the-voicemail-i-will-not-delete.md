It is thirty-four seconds long.

I have listened to it two hundred and eleven times.

I am not counting. I am just telling you.

It is nothing, the message. It was nothing when it arrived — a small, ordinary Tuesday call that I may not have even called back that day. I do not remember if I called back. That is the kind of thing that takes up more room in the chest than it deserves.

But the message.

There is the sound of somewhere familiar in the background. There is the particular way your name sounds when someone who loves you says it — not formally, not completely, just the shape of your name in their mouth, the specific frequency of it, the way no one else has ever said it in exactly that way and no one ever will again.

There is traffic somewhere. A door. Then nothing, then them, then the message, which is ordinary, which is Tuesday, which is just: call me back when you can, and take care of yourself, and — the pause before the last part — and I love you.

Thirty-four seconds.

I have a phone full of photographs.

I have letters.

I have the sweater I will not wash.

But this is the only place where time still moves in the right direction —

where I can press play

and they are still calling,

still waiting,

still saying the ordinary things

that I did not know were everything

until everything was what they were.
