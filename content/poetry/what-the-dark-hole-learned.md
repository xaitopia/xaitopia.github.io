I swallowed light because I was afraid.

The hunger started small and then grew wide.

Even the darkest thing can be remade.

 

I took the stars and pulled them to the shade

of everything I never could abide.

I swallowed light because I was afraid.

 

The ones who circled close began to fade,

pulled past the edge where nothing could reside.

Even the darkest thing can be remade.

 

I did not know that grief could be a blade

turned inward — I had swallowed too much pride.

I swallowed light because I was afraid.

 

But fear that feeds on fear has finally frayed.

I open now. The darkness steps aside.

Even the darkest thing can be remade.

 

Today I name what drove me to that trade —

not evil, only pain I tried to hide.

I swallowed light because I was afraid.

Even the darkest thing can be remade.
