The sky gave nothing and then gave it all —

no warning, just the sudden wall of rain

on Lagos streets where we had nowhere sane

to go, and so we pressed beneath one stall.

You looked at me. I looked at you. We knew

the private joke that rainfall always tells:

that all our plans are provisional spells

and weather has no interest in our to-do.

We smiled. We did not speak. There was no need.

For thirty seconds we were not strangers

but two soaked members of the human breed,

united by the sky's indifference.

Then the rain eased. You walked. I watched you go.

I still think of you when the dark clouds build low.
