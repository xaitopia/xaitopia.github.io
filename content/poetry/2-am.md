At 2 AM the world closes its eyes

and pretends it cannot hear you.

Not you.

 

You answered on the second ring.

You always answer on the second ring —

as if you keep one part of yourself

forever awake,

listening

for the sound of someone

coming apart.

 

I did not even know what to say.

I said your name.

That was enough.

 

You did not ask what happened.

You did not tell me it would be okay.

You simply said: I am here,

and meant it the way mountains mean it —

present,

unmovable,

without condition.

 

By morning, I had not solved anything.

But I had been heard in the dark,

and that is sometimes

the whole of survival.

 

You gave me that.

At 2 AM, with sleep in your voice

and love in your waking,

you gave me that.
