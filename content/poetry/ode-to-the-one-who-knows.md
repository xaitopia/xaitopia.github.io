O blessed friend, who learned the way

before I ever thought to say —

two sugars, oat milk, not too strong,

you knew it all before too long.

 

The way you lift the mug and turn

before I've crossed the threshold — when

the kettle's already singing heat

and you have made the morning sweet.

 

No love is louder than this small

and quiet act of knowing all

the little things that tell you who

a person is — and loving true.

 

Not diamonds set in golden rings,

not grand and sweeping, borrowed things —

but coffee, ready, hot, and made

the way that makes the world less frayed.

 

O keeper of my smallest self,

the part I never put on shelf —

you have memorised the me I am

before the day begins to cram.

 

So here, I raise this cup to you

who knew before I even knew

that love lives in the careful art

of learning someone else by heart.
