They have always been there.

You just need the right angle of light

and the willingness to stop.

Late afternoon, the sun comes low through the window

at exactly that angle —

and suddenly you can see them:

a whole world of small things,

spiralling without urgency

through a column of gold.

They are not going anywhere.

They have no appointment.

They are simply being carried by air

so gentle it does not even have a name.

I think this is what peace looks like

if peace were visible:

a slow revolving in the warmth of something larger,

untroubled by the falling,

because the falling is also a kind of floating.

You could watch for ten minutes.

You could watch for an hour.

Nobody would know.

Nobody would come looking.

The world turns its noise elsewhere

and you stand here,

in a column of gold,

watching the small things spin.

This is available to you.

It has always been available.

You only need to stop

and let the light find the angle

it has been waiting all afternoon to find.
