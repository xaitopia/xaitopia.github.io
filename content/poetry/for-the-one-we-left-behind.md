It was an animal of uncertain species.

Brown, once.

Matted in the way of the truly beloved.

One eye was a button replacement —

the original lost in an incident we do not discuss.

The ear had been repaired twice

and the repair was part of it now,

the stitching as familiar as a scar becomes familiar,

as the body eventually claims every mark made upon it.

We left it somewhere between the hotel and the bus

and the corridor and the morning.

These things happen on trips.

Everyone said so.

Everyone was right and everyone was wrong.

No one told us grief comes in sizes —

that a small grief felt completely

is not a small thing.

That a child standing at a car window,

watching a town disappear,

understanding for the first time

that some things do not come back —

that child is doing something enormous.

That child is learning the shape of loss.

That knowledge, once learned, does not unlearn.

We never replaced it.

You cannot replace it.

There is only ever one first thing you loved

that the world took before you were ready.

Rest, wherever you were left.

You were so loved.

You had one button eye and you were so loved.
