The lines at the corners of the eyes are the years I laughed — the mirror holds.

The grey coming in at the temples is every night survived — the mirror holds.

I used to approach it like an opponent, looking for flaws.

Now I approach it like a witness. The whole story — the mirror holds.

There is the year I was broken. You can see it in the jaw —

how it learned to set itself when the ground moved. The mirror holds.

There is the year I became a mother, a son, a person responsible.

The forehead knows. The eyes know. The mirror holds.

They sell creams and procedures for the softening of evidence.

I will keep my evidence. Every mark is mine — the mirror holds.

The face is not a problem to be solved.

It is a record to be read. All of it — the mirror holds.
