Before the first reed was cut, the river hummed a melody.

The cosmos held its breath, already dreaming of a melody.

 

In empty halls before the musicians came, a melody.

The cedar knew its purpose: to become a melody.

 

The unplucked string still carries what it longs to be — melody.

Before the instrument, the instrument's truest soul: melody.

 

What the universe exhaled on the first cold morning was melody.

No ear existed yet to hear it — still, it was melody.

 

The poet sets the pen down now to receive the oldest melody.

We do not write the song. We only find the melody.
