I wore the hardness like a second skin.

It kept me upright when the cold came through.

But underneath — the softer self begins.

I built the armour in the years of thin,

the years of proving I could make it too.

I wore the hardness like a second skin.

No one could reach me, not even my kin —

I mistook distance for a point of view.

But underneath, the softer self begins

to push against the edges, wearing thin

the shell that once was shelter, now a shoe

I wore. The hardness was a second skin

that fit the child who learned that to be seen

was dangerous. That child was right. She knew.

But underneath — the softer self begins.

The old armour served. I thank it. Then

I set it down. There is nothing left to do

but wear the softness like a second skin.

Underneath — the truer self begins.
