I have forgiven this before. I will again.

The wound reopens when the season turns.

Forgiveness is not a door you lock and then

forget — it is a fire that always burns.

The wound reopens when the season turns,

when certain songs play, or the light falls right.

Forget — it is a fire that always burns,

not out, but warm, a manageable light.

When certain songs play, or the light falls right,

the old thing surfaces and asks its question.

Not out, but warm, a manageable light —

I answer it the same: I made a lesson.

The old thing surfaces and asks its question:

were you not old enough to know better then?

I answer it the same: I made a lesson.

I have forgiven this before. I will again.
