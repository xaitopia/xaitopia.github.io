The hands know what the mind has not yet learned.

Press, fold, and turn. The dough will teach you how.

In bread, I found the quiet thing I'd earned.

The week had left me hollow and concerned —

I came to flour and water, hip to plough.

The hands know what the mind has not yet learned.

No screen, no noise, no answer to be earned,

just warmth beneath my palm and rising now.

In bread, I found the quiet thing I'd earned.

The yeast is old as civilisation, burned

into our bones — this making of a vow.

The hands know what the mind has not yet learned:

that patience is a practice, not conferred

on special souls, but chosen, press by press, brow

to brow with the work. The quiet thing I'd earned

was this: to slow, to fold, to be concerned

with only what is under me right now.

The hands know what the mind has not yet learned.

In bread, I found the quiet thing I'd earned.
