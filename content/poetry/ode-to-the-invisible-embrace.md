O invisible and constant,

O force that holds the coffee in my cup,

that pins the sleeping child to the mattress,

that pulls the rain back down

after the clouds have had their say —

 

I have taken you entirely for granted.

 

Every step I've ever walked,

you were there beneath the sole,

the tireless collaborator

who never asked for credit,

never asked for anything at all

except my weight —

and even that, you held.

 

What would it be without you?

One moment of lost footing

and we'd all just

drift —

coffee and children and raindrops and all of us

slowly separating from each other

in the cold.

 

That is what I think you are:

the universe's quiet insistence

that we stay.

That we remain here,

near the ground,

near each other.

 

The apple knew.

It did not ask to fall.

It simply fell

the way all true things fall —

not down, exactly,

but toward.

 

Toward the earth. Toward the centre.

Toward whatever we mean

when we say

we belong somewhere.

 

O constant pull,

O weight I carry that is also carrying me —

I have been walking on you

my whole life

without a single word of thanks.

 

So here:

thank you

for the cup still on the table,

for the child still in the bed,

for keeping me from floating off

from everything I love.
