We have been sitting here for an hour

and neither of us has spoken

and neither of us minds.

 

The afternoon light slants through the blinds

the way it always has,

and your breathing is the only clock I need.

 

I used to think friendship was noise —

the filling up of rooms,

the reassurance of sound.

 

But here, beside you,

I understand what it means

to be held without being touched,

to be known without being asked.

 

The silence between us

is not empty.

It is every conversation we have ever had,

resting.
