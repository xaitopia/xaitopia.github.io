The leaves release. They do not call it loss.

They burn through amber, rust, and fading red.

To fall with colour is its own kind of grace.

 

What clung all summer earns the right to cross

the open air — to spiral and go ahead.

The leaves release. They do not call it loss.

 

I've watched you hold what seasons tried to toss,

a thing past ripe, kept close, still unfed.

To fall with colour is its own kind of grace.

 

The tree is not the lesser for the cost

of emptying. The branches open instead.

The leaves release. They do not call it loss.

 

We name it ending. We pronounce it frost.

But look — the ground is gold beneath your tread.

To fall with colour is its own kind of grace.

 

Release the thing past season. All that dross

you've called yourself — let go of what's misled.

The leaves release. They do not call it loss.

To fall with colour is its own kind of grace.
