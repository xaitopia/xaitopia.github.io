O green and patient ones,

you who ask for so little —

only water, only light,

only the occasional turning of the pot

so all your sides get their Sunday.

I come to you on weekend mornings

with a watering can and a pair of scissors

and something that is not quite prayer

but is not quite not prayer either.

I pinch the yellow leaf before it spreads its sadness.

I loosen the soil with one careful finger.

I hold the misting bottle close

and let the fine spray fall

like something I couldn't manage to say during the week.

What is it I am actually doing here?

The botanist would say: maintaining turgor pressure,

enabling photosynthesis, preventing root rot.

All of these are true.

But what I am doing is tending.

And tending is what I do

when I do not know what else to do —

when the week has been a thing

that cannot be undone,

only moved through,

and I need to put my hands on something alive

and help it.

So here I am.

O green and patient ones —

thank you for needing so little.

Thank you for growing anyway.

Thank you for being here

on Sunday mornings

when I need something to tend.
