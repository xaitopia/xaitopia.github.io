I fold the things I was into a box.

The room grows bare. It does not know me now.

The walls remember nothing of the locks

I kept on every feeling, every vow.

The room grows bare. It does not know me now —

the posters peeled, the circles left behind.

I kept on every feeling, every vow

the evidence of who I used to mind.

The posters peeled, the circles left behind

where sticky tack held hope against the wall.

The evidence of who I used to mind

was smaller than I thought, packed into all

where sticky tack held hope against the wall.

Outside, the compound smells of harmattan.

Was smaller than I thought, packed into all

these boxes, this whole childhood, this one man

outside, the compound smells of harmattan,

and I am boxing up the years I kept.

These boxes, this whole childhood, this one man

I was — I fold him gently, having wept.

I am boxing up the years I kept.

The walls remember nothing of the locks.

I was — I fold him gently, having wept.

I fold the things I was into a box.
