The silence out beyond the furthest stars

is not the silence of an empty room —

not what remains between the lost guitar

and voice, not grief's particular hollow flume.

 

This silence has a texture and a weight,

a woolly press of dark that holds you near,

the way two hands when opened simply wait —

not empty, but so full there's nothing here

 

to fear. I think the cosmos built that hush

not as a void to swallow human sound,

but as the drawn breath held before the rush

of music — patient, present, and profound.

 

It shaped that silence like an open door:

so we might find we're held, and ask no more.
