The world is far too wide for wings so new.

You tremble in the damp and heavy air,

But you'll learn to lean into the morning light.

 

The dark was safe and small and held you true,

Now colors ache with weight you cannot bear;

The world is far too wide for wings so new.

 

Your papered veins are soaked in silver dew,

A fragile ghost of who was once just there,

But you'll learn to lean into the morning light.

 

Be patient with the pulse that's pushing through,

The sky is a heavy burden, a brilliant snare;

The world is far too wide for wings so new.

 

The wind is whispering what you must do,

To shake the ghost of silk from out your hair,

And you'll learn to lean into the morning light.

 

So trust the gold that's soaking into you,

The beauty that you didn't know was there.

The world is far too wide for wings so new;

You'll learn to lean into the morning light.
