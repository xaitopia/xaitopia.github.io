I was certain. Do not argue with me now.

The moon was following our car that night —

I watched through glass and every time we'd bow

around a corner, there it was: still bright,

still there, still looking at me through the pane.

I told my father. He said: yes, I see.

He did not explain. I am glad. Explain

too early and you lose the mystery

that makes a child feel chosen by the sky.

I grew and learned the science of it — sure,

the parallax, the distance, the goodbye

to magic that an explanation cures.

But something kept: on long night drives, alone,

I check the window. There it is. Still home.
