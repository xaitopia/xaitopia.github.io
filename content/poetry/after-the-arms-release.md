You pulled away before I was prepared.

The arms release; the warmth is slower to go —

it lingers at the shoulder, in the careful

architecture of a chest pressed close.

The skin remembers even when the eyes

have lost the shape of you to the crowd.

There is a physics to the goodbyes

we do not choose: warmth has a half-life, proud

and stubborn, staying past its invitation.

An hour later, cooking, I will find

it still there — a ghost of close relation,

a warmth the afternoon failed to unbind.

Some people leave a room but leave their heat.

Love finds the skin a long time past the street.
