We have built a country between us over the years,

and it has no borders you could draw on a map.

Its currency is a single look.

Its national anthem is a sentence we started in 2014

and have never finished,

because the punchline arrived before the joke did

and we never recovered.

 

We have words for things that have no words in English —

a specific kind of Wednesday-afternoon sadness,

the particular joy of finding money in an old coat pocket,

the feeling when a song comes on

and we both stop breathing at the same time.

 

Tourists cannot visit. There is no phrase book.

We once laughed for forty minutes in a waiting room

at something so obscure

that explaining it would require at least three years

of shared history and a diagram.

People watched us. We did not care.

 

This is what happens when two people

have enough time together:

they become their own civilisation,

with their own mythology,

their own inside names for strangers on trains,

their own shorthand for love.

 

I am fluent in exactly one person.

That person is you.
